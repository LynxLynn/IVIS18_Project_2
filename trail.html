Data Gathering

The goal of WVS is to gather data and show that data to the world, by asking people what they think about certain values and beliefs. I went through that survey data with the quest to find some interesting variables and ended up with data from eight variables for 36 chosen countries over the years 1995-2014.

Chosen countries: Aims of country: first choice, Environment vs economic growth, Confidence: The armed forces, Employment status, State of health, Feeling of happiness, Trust in people, Income equality


With this, I want to creat a visualization where you can easily see and analyze data from different countries.

Analytic Trail 1

To get a good overview of the data I had collected I started with a map of the world, where the countries with available data is marked with a blue color(See Fig.1). If we “zoom-in” and investigate the map further we can see that hovering over a country give you it’s name(See Fig.2), and by clicking on it a table with the available data is presented just below the map(See Fig.3). By swapping country and variables you can see and compare the different answers throughout the years. I thought it could be, and found it interesting to compare each variable per country with the world average of each variable. Are they similar to each other? or is there a huge different when it comes to certain questions? So,as you can see in figure 4, just below the table of data for each country you can click on a specific variable and get a graph with the world average. With the two different data sets showing, you can compare and see if there are any big differences(See Fig.4).


Analytic Trail 2

(Not implemented in visualization)

Data from one source it great, but to be able to compare that data with another source is even greater. So to make the collected data more interesting I (hypothetically) combined my WVS data with data from Gapminder.
